About

Hello, I’m Richelle. That’s a ‘Michelle’ with an R. Currently a computer science major and biology minor, I have a particular interest in the intersection of technology and biology. At the Stony Brook University, I have an internship doing data analysis with the Grow More Foundation, a new, non-profit organization that promotes awareness about genetically engineered foods. Because I have a love for spreading and receiving knowledge, I have also previously worked as a teacher’s assistant at Girls Who Code, as well as a teacher’s assistant exploring the use of virtual reality in theater.

The prospect of biotechnology is exploding with exciting potential and I am looking for ways to use my computer science skills to contribute to the scientific community. Under my belt I have skills ranging from technical coding languages to web development to design (see my resume here). But I’m also particularly enthusiastic about exploring new things and seeing how it all fits together to better biotechnology.

I like to meet and talk about things with new people. See below for contact info.
